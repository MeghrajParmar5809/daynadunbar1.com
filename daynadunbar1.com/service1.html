Private Sessions

These sessions take place in person or on Zoom. Beginning with an invocation to call in Spirit’s assistance, Dayna creates a safe container for the deep exploration of the source of the client’s suffering. Approaching the work as a deeply nurturing presence as well as a detective of the psyche, Dayna zeroes in on the mental and emotional knots that keep her clients imprisoned in inner struggle.

Dayna calls her approach Two Pillars of Awakening and Healing—Love and Wisdom. She offers the alchemy that occurs when we meet our humanity fully in love while we also do the waking up work offered by perennial spiritual Wisdom. In this way, she supports the shift in identity from a separate self-structure to that of Infinite Being and Oneness in Spirit.

Personal Shamanic Intensives

In a core three-day experience, Dayna actively works with clients on the memories and conditioning that have caused their suffering. Using this modality, which combines shamanic journey work with spiritual and therapeutic counseling, optimizes the healing of depression, anxiety, PTSD, addictions, phobias, OCD, fear of dying for terminal patients, as well as plateauing on the awakening path.

This core three-day experience with Dayna includes:

  • -A preparatory counseling session of about 90 minutes the day before the journey. The two initial sessions allow Dayna to understand the origins of the client’s mental, emotional and physical suffering and establish therapeutic safety and connection.

  • -A full day shamanic journey in which Dayna actively counsels the client based on the material that has arisen in the previous sessions. This lasts approximately six hours, from 10am to 4pm.


  • A full day of integration (10am to 4pm) the day after the journey. This consists of guided meditation, breathwork, chanting (if desired), somatic practices like gentle yoga, going over her notes from the day before, how to anchor the learnings and next steps in the client’s life, as well as integration counseling.

  • Dayna also types up the journey notes and sends them to the client.

Depending on the level of trauma or issues being faced, Dayna will often require counseling leading up to this three-day experience. This establishes important safety and connection and helps Dayna understand and begin working with long-standing core wounds and beliefs.

Personal Residential Intensives

These are four nights and five days in residence with Dayna. The client can either travel to be with Dayna in her home in Arkansas, in Mexico or Dayna can travel to the client’s home. These intensives include the three-day shamanic journey experience as well as more guided meditation, breathwork, energy healing, somatic practices, tour guidance, visiting sacred sites together, meals and more.

A recent example of a personal intensive:

Z.S. flew in to join me in my home in the beautiful Ozark Mountains of Northwest Arkansas. Everyone who comes here, and I mean everyone, is shocked by the natural beauty, art and culture. I picked her up and took her to lunch at the lovely Eleven Café at Crystal Bridges Museum, which is a spiritual destination in itself. (It opened on 11/11/11). We walked around the grounds, soaking up all the giant crystals and outdoor sculptures, and being in the healing vibration of such natural beauty.

We then came to my home and had a two-hour prep session for the shamanic journey. That night, I made dinner and we watched spiritual entertainment together. I gave her all the necessary supplements to support the work.

On the day of her journey, in the morning, I guided her in pranayama and yoga with hands on activations. I then led her into a deep meditation. The journey was utterly profound, but it is best if you hear about it from her. See her feedback below. That evening, I made dinner and we relaxed then went to bed early.

The following day, integration, I guided her in pranayama, yoga and meditation then we sat in the sunshine on my deck and went over her notes to anchor in the immense shifts in consciousness and energy she had experienced the day before. We went to a sound bath and meditation at the Momentary, another fantastic museum here then I took her to lunch. After that, we went and sat by a crystal-clear turquoise stream in the sunshine and completed her integration counseling. Then I drove her to a spa where she got a 90-minute massage with my favorite massage therapist in the area.

The next morning, we did more yoga, breathwork and meditation on the deck then celebrated with kirtan chanting and dancing. Then I drove her to the airport. She reports that her reentry into her old but completely new life was easy.
